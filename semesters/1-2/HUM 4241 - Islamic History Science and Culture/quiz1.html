3
At age 35, before his prophethood, the Prophet (PBUH) resolved the famous Hajar al-Aswad (Black Stone) dispute among the Quraysh tribes without bloodshed. Describe:
(a) what the dispute was about · (b) the specific solution he proposed · (c) what this reveals about his leadership
▾
Answer
(a) The Dispute

During the reconstruction of the Ka'bah, prominent Quraysh tribes could not agree on which clan should have the immense honor of placing the Black Stone back into its corner. The argument escalated, threatening a bloody inter-tribal war within Makkah.

(b) The Solution

The Prophet (PBUH) placed his cloak on the ground, set the Black Stone in the center, and instructed a representative chief from each competing tribe to lift a corner of the cloth together. Once raised to the correct height, the Prophet (PBUH) placed the stone into the wall with his own hands — giving every clan an equal share of the honor.

(c) Leadership Qualities Revealed

Exceptional wisdom, absolute impartiality, superb diplomatic tact, and conflict-resolution skills — all before receiving revelation. He possessed a unique ability to unite opposing factions and commanded deep moral authority, earning the trust of all Makkah's leaders.

Persecution, Migration & Constitution
7
Analyse the five stages of Quraysh's persecution of early Muslims. For EACH stage, provide one specific example. Then: what two major personal losses did the Prophet (PBUH) suffer in the Year of Sorrow, and how did he continue his mission?
▾
Answer
Stage 1 — Verbal Abuse & Mockery

The Quraysh falsely labelled the Prophet (PBUH) as a sorcerer, poet, or madman.

Stage 2 — Physical Torture of Vulnerable Converts

Bilal ibn Rabah was tortured under heavy rocks; Sumayyah was martyred.

Stage 3 — Social & Economic Boycott

The entire Banu Hashim clan was forced into starvation inside the valley of She'eb Abi Talib for three years.

Stage 4 — Persecution Beyond Makkah

Migrations to Abyssinia; the Prophet (PBUH) was physically assaulted at Ta'if.

Stage 5 — Assassination Plot

The Dar al-Nadwa conspiracy had all clans agree to murder the Prophet collectively, forcing the Hijrah.

Year of Sorrow (10th year of Prophethood) — Two Losses:

① Death of Khadijah (RA) — his primary internal emotional support.
② Death of Abu Talib — his primary external political protector.

Despite these losses, the Prophet (PBUH) shifted focus to external preaching among Arab tribes during Hajj seasons, which led to the historic Pledges of Aqabah with the Ansar and paved the way for the Islamic state in Madinah.

9
What was the Charter of Madinah (Sahifat al-Madinah), and why is it described as the world's first written constitution? Name any THREE specific rights or obligations it guaranteed.
▾
Answer

The Charter of Madinah was a landmark formal document drafted by the Prophet (PBUH) upon his arrival in the city. It is recognized as the world's first written constitution because it systematically established a framework of legal rights, duties, and responsibilities for Muslims, Jews, and all local tribes — forming a unified, pluralistic state structure from heterogeneous communities.

Three Guaranteed Rights / Obligations:

① Complete preservation of religious freedom for all distinct groups.
② A binding commitment to the collective defense of the city against external threats.
③ All major disputes must be referred to the Prophet (PBUH) for final resolution.

▾
Answer

50 designated archers abandoned their critical defensive positions on Mount Uhud — driven by the illusion of early success and a desire for worldly spoils — directly violating the Prophet's explicit strategic military command.

Battle of Khandaq

5 AH · 627 CE

13
The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah appeared to be a defeat for Muslims, yet Allah called it "A Clear Victory" (Quran 48:1). List THREE seemingly unfavourable terms, then explain TWO concrete reasons why it proved to be a strategic triumph.
▾
Answer

Three Unfavourable Terms:

① Muslims had to return to Madinah without performing Umrah that year.
② They were restricted to returning the following year for a maximum of three days only.
③ Any Quraysh apostate fleeing to Madinah must be returned — but Muslim apostates fleeing to Makkah would not be returned.

Two Reasons It Was a Strategic Triumph:

① A ten-year ceasefire allowed Islam to spread rapidly in peace — the Muslim population doubled in two years, yielding more conversions than in the prior 18 years of conflict.
② Diplomatic stability allowed the Prophet (PBUH) to dispatch formal letters to major global rulers — Byzantine, Persian, Ethiopian, and Egyptian empires — establishing Islam on the world stage.
"Indeed, We have granted you a clear triumph." Surah Al-Fath · Quran 48:1

"The Farewell Sermon was the world's first universal declaration of human rights — 1,316 years before the UN Charter of 1948." Critically evaluate: (a) ≥4 key messages with Quranic evidence · (b) compare ONE message to UN law · (c) explain Quran 5:3 and its theological significance.
▾
Answer

(a) Four Key Messages of the Farewell Sermon:

① Sanctity of Life & Property

Supported by Quran 17:33 — forbids taking an innocent life.

② Complete Racial Equality

"An Arab has no superiority over a non-Arab." Aligned with Quran 49:13 — "the most noble is the most God-fearing."

③ Prohibition of Economic Exploitation (Riba)

Aligned with Quran 2:275 — prohibiting interest-based transactions.

④ Protection of Women's Rights

Aligned with Quran 4:19 — "live with them in kindness."

(b) Comparison to Modern International Law

The message of racial and human equality directly mirrors Articles 1 & 2 of the UN Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948) — "all human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights, without distinction of any kind."

(c) Quran 5:3 — Theological Completion of Islam
"Today I have perfected your religion for you and completed My favor upon you and have approved Islam as your religion." Surah Al-Ma'idah · Quran 5:3 · Revealed at Farewell Hajj, 10 AH

This verse marks the absolute theological completion of Islam — its spiritual laws, ethical boundaries, and guidance for human civilization were fully finalized for mankind, requiring no subsequent divine revelations.
